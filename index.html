Tongyu QIU

Uploading knowledge to the world.

I’m a psychology and public health researcher interested in how science and technology can expand access to knowledge and improve mental health. Currently an MPH student in Social and Behavioral Sciences at Yale, my work sits at the intersection of psychology, computational methods, and implementation science.

I study mental health across the lifespan and develop analytic and AI-assisted research tools that make scientific discovery more scalable, transparent, and reproducible. I’m particularly interested in how emerging technologies, including large language models, can support research synthesis, evidence-based decision-making, and the translation of scientific knowledge into real-world impact.

About Me

My research is driven by a simple question: how can we make scientific knowledge more useful, more accessible, and more actionable for the people who need it most? I am especially interested in using rigorous psychological and public health research to address real-world mental health challenges, while also exploring how new technologies can help bridge the gap between knowledge generation and practical impact.

Across my work, I combine psychological theory, quantitative methods, and AI-assisted tools to study mental health, improve research efficiency, and support the broader dissemination of evidence. I hope to contribute to a future in which science is not limited to academic spaces, but is translated into forms that are understandable, scalable, and meaningful in everyday life.
